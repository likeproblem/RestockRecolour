# Parts Completed: 171/340 [50.29%]

This Wiki details the parts which RestockRecolour will be working with.  Because of the complicated nature of this mod, this list consists of **ALL** parts that would appear if a user were to install Kerbal Space Program, its expansions, Breaking Ground and Making History, and the mods Restock and Restock+.  The parts which are specifically affected by Restock/Restock+ are indicated with **BOLD PART NAMES**, while parts that Restock/Restock+ does not affect (aside from possibly adding depthmasks) are indicated with _italics_

For the most part, _Stock/BG/MH Parts_ will have existing TURD configs, either from TheMan WithNoName (creator of TURD for stock) or OnlyLightMatters (the creator of the TURDs for the 2 expansions).  

_________________________________________________________________________________
**KEY**
_________________________________________________________________________________

* 🔲  Restock/Restock+ Part without a recolor

* ✔️  Restock/Restock+ Part with completed recolor

* ✔️❗  Restock/Restock+ Part with completed recolor that has some kind of variant issue that needs fixing 

* 🔲❗ Restock/Restock+ Part without a recolor that has false TURD recolour, will need **!MODULE[KSPTextureSwitch],* {}**
____________________________________________________________________________________

* --  Stock/MH/BG Part that has working TURD configs compatible with TU_Restock

* ❓  Stock/MH/BG Part that has broken TURD configs, some incompatibility with TU_Restock

* ⬜ Stock/MH/BG Part that _NEVER_ had a TURD config in the first place

* ✅  Stock/MH/BG Part that _had_ broken TURD configs, which were fixed, & parts that never had a recolor config that now do.
____________________________________________________________________________________
**ADDITIONAL INDICATORS**

* ✴️ This mark indicates a part with _multiple_ variants, and it means that ALL of those variants are confirmed to have working recolors.

* 🔄 Indicates a part which has **working** recolors that is entirely missing recolors for one or more of its variants.  Such parts _may_ call for the more complicated 'VariantSwitch'-style recolors or Spartwo's method.  These parts need more attention.
